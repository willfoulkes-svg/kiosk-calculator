How to use this in a commercial conversation

  1. Pick the country first. Card processing costs differ significantly - US and UK in particular. If the country is wrong, the Net Fee numbers are wrong.
  2. Fill in the customer profile once. Two sets of volumes (OO GMV/AOV, POS GMV/AOV), their current commission + processing rates, and the two per-order fees (OO end-user fee, POS transaction fee). Orders are calculated for you.
  3. Enter the new rates you're thinking about. New OO commission, new POS processing, new subscription. The hero shows the monthly + annual uplift.
  4. "Where to prioritise price increases" tells you - for this specific customer - which lever moves the needle more per +0.1%. Use this to decide which rate to push on first in renewal talks.
  5. The matrix shows the full solution space. The blue-highlighted cell is your current plan; green cells are better, red cells are worse. Useful when you're negotiating trade-offs ("can we give them back 0.1% OO if we gain 0.2% POS?").
  6. Note: this is a rate-sensitivity tool, not a margin guardrail. For discount approval, use the POS or Kiosk Discount Approval calculators.

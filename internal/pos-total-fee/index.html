What does Flipdish actually earn from a POS deal each month? The processing fee and transaction fee you type in are what the merchant pays — Flipdish only keeps what's left after our card processing cost. This calculator shows you two numbers: Net Fees (what the rates alone earn) and Software Net Revenue (Net Fees plus the monthly subscription).

How to use this

  1. The big idea: the processing fee and transaction fee you type in are what the merchant pays. Flipdish only keeps the difference between that and our card processing cost. That difference is your Net Fees. The subscription is layered on top to give you Software Net Revenue.
  2. Country first. Card processing costs differ per country — UK is 0.70% + £0.006 per order, Ireland is 0.52% + €0.008 per order.
  3. Fill in the customer profile. Monthly POS GMV, AOV, processing fee %, transaction fee, monthly subscription. Estimated orders per month is calculated for you (GMV ÷ AOV).
  4. Two hero numbers. Net Fees = what the rates alone earn, after card processing cost is taken out. Software Net Revenue = Net Fees plus the monthly subscription. The breakdown below shows every line that feeds them.
  5. The matrix shows Net Fees at every (processing fee, transaction fee) combo — sub deliberately excluded so you can see if the rates alone earn money. Red cells mean those rates lose money on every transaction; only the subscription would be propping the deal up. Use this to spot when discounts on the rates push the deal underwater.
